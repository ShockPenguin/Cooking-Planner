## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until just shy of al dente.
2. While the pasta cooks, thinly slice the garlic. Warm olive oil in a wide pan over low heat, add the garlic and red pepper flakes, and cook gently until the garlic is pale gold — do not let it brown.
3. Reserve a mug of pasta water. Drain the pasta and add it to the pan with a splash of the starchy water. Toss vigorously over low heat until the sauce emulsifies and coats every strand.
4. Finish with chopped parsley and plenty of black pepper. Taste for salt.

## Notes

- Browning the garlic turns the dish bitter — pull the pan off the heat the moment you catch the toasted-garlic aroma.
- A peeler of parmesan on top is traditionally frowned upon but delicious.
