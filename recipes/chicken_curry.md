## Instructions

1. Start the rice cooking (2 cups rice, ~3.5 cups water, pinch of salt).
2. Finely chop onions, mince garlic and ginger. Cut chicken into bite-sized pieces and salt generously.
3. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Cook onions with a pinch of salt until deeply golden — 8–10 minutes. Don't rush this; it's the flavor base.
4. Add garlic and ginger, stir 1 minute. Add curry powder and toast for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add the chicken, stirring to coat in the spices, and cook until the outside turns opaque.
6. Pour in the canned tomatoes (crush with spoon) and coconut milk. Simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chicken is tender and the sauce has thickened.
7. Taste for salt, finish with chopped cilantro, and serve over rice.

## Notes

- Swap chicken breast for thighs if you prefer — more forgiving and richer.
- A spoon of plain yogurt or a squeeze of lemon at the end brightens it up.
