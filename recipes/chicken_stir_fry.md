## Instructions

1. Rinse the rice and start it cooking in a rice cooker or pot (2 cups rice, ~3.5 cups water, pinch of salt).
2. Slice chicken into bite-sized pieces and toss with 1 tbsp soy sauce. Mince garlic and ginger. Cut bell pepper into strips and break broccoli into florets.
3. Heat a large wok or skillet over high heat until smoking. Add a splash of neutral oil and the chicken in a single layer. Sear without moving for 1 minute, then stir-fry until just cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
4. Add garlic and ginger to the pan, then broccoli and bell pepper. Stir-fry 2–3 minutes until crisp-tender, adding a splash of water if anything threatens to burn.
5. Return chicken to the pan. Add remaining soy sauce and sesame oil. Toss to coat, taste for salt, and serve over rice.

## Notes

- High heat is the whole point — don't crowd the pan.
- Adds that work well: a drizzle of oyster sauce, a handful of cashews, or a pinch of sugar to balance the soy.
