## Instructions

1. Warm the stock in a saucepan and keep it at a bare simmer. Finely dice the onion, mince the garlic, slice the mushrooms.
2. In a wide, heavy pan, melt 1 tbsp butter with the olive oil. Sauté mushrooms over high heat until deeply browned, season with salt, and set aside.
3. Lower heat to medium. Add another tbsp butter, then the onion, cooking until soft and translucent (~5 min). Add garlic for the last minute.
4. Stir in the rice and toast for 1–2 minutes until the edges look translucent. Pour in the wine and stir until it's almost fully absorbed.
5. Begin adding the warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring frequently and letting each addition absorb before adding the next. This takes 18–20 minutes. Taste as you go — the rice should be al dente, the texture loose and creamy.
6. Off the heat, stir in the mushrooms, remaining butter, grated parmesan, and chopped parsley. Rest 2 minutes covered, then taste for salt and pepper and serve immediately.

## Notes

- Risotto waits for no one — serve the moment it's done or it'll stiffen up.
- A spoon dragged through the finished risotto should leave a slow-closing trail, not a cleared lane.
