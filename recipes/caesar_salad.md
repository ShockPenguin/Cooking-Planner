## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 200°C / 400°F. Tear bread into rough chunks, toss with 1 tbsp olive oil and a pinch of salt, and bake 8–10 minutes until deeply golden.
2. Wash and dry the romaine well. Tear or chop into bite-sized pieces.
3. In a large bowl, mash the anchovies and garlic to a paste with a fork. Whisk in the egg yolk, a squeeze of lemon, and plenty of black pepper. Drizzle in the remaining olive oil while whisking until emulsified. Grate in half the parmesan.
4. Add the romaine and toss thoroughly so every leaf is coated. Top with croutons and the remaining parmesan.

## Notes

- The raw egg yolk is traditional; swap for a spoon of mayo if you prefer.
- This dressing lives on leftover roast chicken — don't throw the extras out.
